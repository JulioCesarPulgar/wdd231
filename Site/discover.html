History of Cabimas

Cabimas is a city on the eastern shore of Lake Maracaibo in Zulia state, Venezuela. Historically linked to the oil industry, the city grew rapidly during the 20th century. Today Cabimas blends industrial heritage with coastal landscapes and a resilient local culture.

Demographics

Cabimas is home to a diverse population with strong ties to the petroleum sector, fishing, and local commerce. The city serves as a regional hub for nearby towns.

  • Estimated population: ~250,000 (varies by source)
  • Primary languages: Spanish
  • Key sectors: energy, commerce, services
